Section 01

The Problem

Government search is stuck in the past — AI is widening the gap every day.

{003}

For Most Gov Sites, It's Still 2006

20 years later search is better, but not a lot better. Today users want answers, not links.

WHAT USERS GET
mn.gov/search
WHAT USERS EXPECT
chatgpt.com
Search has gotten better on most gov sites, but fundamentally the same as 20 years ago. On the left: what citizens now expect. On the right: what they get. Since ChatGPT launched in November 2022, the public's mental model of "search" changed permanently.
{004}

Your Citizens Are Living in 2026

They're asking ChatGPT or Gemini instead of searching your site.

The answers they get back are pretty good.

But they come from third-party vendor sites, not your .gov site.

The answers can be factually incorrect.

Tim's car sale / DMV anecdote. Your citizens are already using AI to find your content, you just don't know it. You no longer control the answer — but you can start to take it back.
{005}

The question isn't whether this is happening.

It's whether your agency controls the answers — on your site and off.

This is the core tension. Citizens are getting answers about your services from AI — answers you didn't write, can't verify, and can't update. The question is whether you take control of that.

Keyword Search

You search. You hunt.

Returns a list ranked by keyword relevance

User does all the interpretive work

Where almost every gov site is today

Familiar. Cheap. Often deeply frustrating.

Semantic Search

You search. It understands.

Understands intent, not just keywords

Finds the right page even if exact words don't match

Still returns links — not answers

Low risk. Achievable now. No generative AI.

Meaning, not just words

kayak registration
STAGE 1 0 results found

No pages contain the exact phrase "kayak registration"

kayak registration
STAGE 2 Boat & Watercraft Registration

It knew what you meant.

Not really "AI Search" — no generative AI involved. Vector embeddings match meaning, not vocabulary. The gap between how citizens ask questions and how government titles its pages is enormous — semantic search closes most of it.

Understand the Barriers

The limitations are real.

🗃 Content Sprawl 40 agencies, independent content. AI inherits all of it.
⚖ Policy & Legal Your state is establishing AI governance right now.
🕑 Procurement Lag 6-month contracts vs. 3-month AI release cycles.
🤝 Staff Capacity 1-2 people running an entire state portal.
🚀 Speed of Change AI is moving faster than mortals can comprehend.
💰 Budget Reality New tools compete with existing maintenance.
States are standing up AI governance frameworks now — know what's coming in your state before your pilot gets ahead of it. Each barrier is real, but none are permanent.
{015}

You Can't AI Your Way Out of Bad Content

Outdated content — Last reviewed 2019. AI cites every stale page confidently.

Orphaned pages — Five CMS migrations deep. Nobody owns them.

Org-chart IA — Built around departments, not citizen tasks.

Under-resourced — 1-2 people running an entire state portal.

Bolt an LLM onto a broken content foundation and you get a confident, well-spoken wrong answer.

In government, that's a liability.

AI doesn't fix bad content — it retrieves it, generates from it, and cites it confidently. Stale permit fee from 2019 — AI tells citizens the wrong amount with a source citation.
{016}

Fix Your Foundation

Before you add AI, fix what it will read.

🗑 Kill Dead Weight Archive or remove. Every stale page is retrieval noise.
📋 Content Audit Map what's current, stale, redundant. Figure out your PDFs.
🛠 Fix Structure Content types, clean taxonomies, citizen-centered IA.
📚 Govern It Assign owners. Set review cycles. Enforce standards.
🔍 Search Analytics Know what citizens search for and what returns nothing.
❓ Patch or Rebuild? Are you fixing legacy debt, or does this need a clean start?

AI search quality is directly proportional to content quality.

More sites need a full rebuild than their owners want to admit. Content audit is the one thing you can start this week with no approval needed.

{018}

Path 1 — Purpose-Built SaaS

e.g. Cludo, Coveo, and other up-and-comers

Pros
  • Stage 3 & 4 capable now
  • Gov-specific features, analytics, admin UI
  • No internal dev capacity needed
  • Supported and maintained
Cons
  • Expensive — enterprise subscription pricing
  • Crawler-based — not tied to your Drupal content
  • Data sovereignty — where do citizen queries go?
  • Vendor lock-in — switching is painful
Very few vendors doing this completely for gov right now. Crawler-based means they index your public site — not connected to Drupal's content layer. If you need Stage 3/4 now and don't have dev capacity, this is the only realistic path today.
{019}

Path 2 — You May Already Have It

Microsoft Azure · Google Vertex AI · AWS

Pros
  • Likely inside your existing enterprise agreement
  • FedRAMP authorized — procurement done
  • Drupal integration paths exist for all three
  • Stage 3 capable with proper configuration
Cons
  • Not turnkey — requires real config and dev work
  • Gov IT may own the contract — web team needs access
  • Still requires the same content foundation work

Ask your IT department before signing a new vendor contract. You may already be paying for this.

Azure AI Search has a Search API module for Drupal. Google Vertex AI Search also has one. The middle path is most underused because web teams don't know what's in their agency's enterprise agreements.
{020}

Path 3 — Build Drupal-Native

Drupal AI + Search API + Vector DB + LLM

Pros
  • You own the architecture and the data
  • Tightly coupled to structured Drupal content
  • No vendor lock-in — swap LLM providers
  • Lowest long-term cost, highest control
Cons
  • Requires capable Drupal team + AI expertise
  • Middleware layer: chunking, embedding, pipeline
  • Ecosystem still maturing — you own maintenance
Drupal's structured content and APIs are a genuine head start. The 48+ provider integrations mean you can swap LLMs without rebuilding. Most control, lowest long-term cost, most upfront investment.
{021}

The Content Foundation Requirement

Whichever path you choose:

Your content has to be current.
Your content has to be structured.
Your content has to be governed.

AI doesn't fix bad content.
It retrieves it. Generates from it. Cites it. Confidently.

This is the point worth repeating. Every vendor will tell you their platform handles content quality — none of them do. The content work is yours regardless of path.
